    Preface

    The Holland Festival is the oldest and largest performing arts festival in the Netherlands. It takes place every June in Amsterdam. It comprises theatre, music, opera and modern dance. In recent years, multimedia, visual arts, film and architecture were added to the festival roster.
    One of the events of Holland Festival, The Ways of Water is a group exhibition hosted by Holland Festival and its purpose is to emphasize the connection between water and human kinds through different types of artworks. This group exhibition features 8 different artists.
    Our goal is to create an experimental microsite for post exhibition with an artist page so they can explore the 8 different artists on the exhibition.

    Content design

    Content Design

    The microsite's purpose is to serve as an immersive archive for the audience to relive and explore further. Users will be able to access the site by scanning a QR code distributed after the event. The microsite's ultimate aim is to recreate the sensation of re-attending the exhibition after the fact. The microsite is more experimental in nature, and users can investigate it to simulate the experience of visiting an exhibition.
    The microsite contain two pages which is the landing page and artist page. Upon landing, the user is greeted with a lobby of sorts, where they are introduced to the page with hints on how to navigate. On this page, they can explore the 8 different artists on the exhibition. The artist page is a more detailed page where the user can explore the different works they created in this exhibition. Here, they can also find more information about each piece.

    Adapted qualities from the giants

    We produced insights to drive the visual form of our client's project by investigating and mastering the graphic design processes and philosophies of Dan Friedman and Ellen Lupton. In order to promote study into the composition, the project investigates the use of layering and negative space to selectively display material.

    Art Directions

    By using superimposing image and layering to create dimension and depth in the composition while overlapping elements to draw connections.     Microsite elements

    Deconstructing compositions

    This animation makes use of the dynamic behavior of lines to create a smooth transition into the next page. This unique interaction makes it clear to the user that they are being moved to a new page.
    The hover which displays the masked image in the text from our art direction allows the user to know that the area is interactable.

    Denial + Reward for immersion

    The use of denial and reward creates an immersive journey and simulate the experience of browsing an exhibition. We purposely kept the browsing hint vague, as we intent to let the users explore different methods to maneuver across the canvas. They can drag, scroll, bring the cursor out of frame, or use arrow keys.

    Navigating to other pages

    The top navigation bar can help users locate themselves within the microsite flow, but it can also help users who are not interested in exploring through the whole page to directly access the page they’re looking for.

    Mini map to access sections

    The mini-map serves as a secondary navigation to allow users to quickly access their desired section on the page, or even use as a ‘map’ to guide them to the desired area.

    Hover to reveal

    Hover is used throughout our microsite to make viewing more convenient and interactive, as it reveals extra information and keeps the busy composition clean and less overwhelming.

    Expand to explore details

    When the user clicks on the desired image, the image will expand, so they can take a closer look at the artwork. The extra information makes this action more rewarding.
